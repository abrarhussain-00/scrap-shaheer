About Shakeel Traders Group

Shakeel Traders Group, Pakistan is a family owned and operated business. Based in Gujranwala, Pakistan, with international operations, we strive to be a reliable, honest and dynamic partner for US domestic as well as international markets including (but not limited to) the Americas, Europe, UK, Asia, China, India, Pakistan and the Middle East.

We deliver high standard of quality business to our suppliers and a great value for their money to our buyers in Asia and Far east. We operate 3 yards with furnaces to recycle between 5000 - 7000 MT scrap materials per month, providing the financial backing and stability for global operations.

We are Bureau of International Recycling (BIR) Gold member and ISO 14001-2004 and ISO 9001-2000 certified. Our company also strives for being environmentally friendly and operates under the guidelines set forth by the Environmental Protection Agency under the Government of Pakistan.

Our Rich History

Shakeel Traders Group Pakistan was founded in 1951 by Muhammad Siddique as a very small scale local brokerage firm specializing in all kinds of scrap materials in Gujranwala, Pakistan. Through his honesty and integrity Muhammad Siddique grew his clientele from small scale factories to large industries in Pakistan.

His son Muhammad Shakeel joined the business in early 1973 and grew it worldwide. The company started importing non-ferrous scrap materials from Europe and the United Kingdom. Mostly the business consisted of buying the scrap materials from these countries and selling it domestically in the Pakistani scrap market where it was smelted and used in various industrial sectors. The company performed the middle-man role till late 70's.

Through years of trading Muhammad Shakeel, grew the business to include Europe and the United Kingdom to the United States, Canada, Scandinavia, Eastern Europe and Middle East. The company started dealing with ferrous scrap in addition to non-ferrous scrap only.

In the early 80's the company made a big investment and purchased 300 acres of land, where they constructed their own yards to store the scrap material. Additionally they put up furnaces to process some of the materials themselves. Muhammad Shakeel not only headed Shakeel Traders Group, but held various social and business leadership position including The Chairman of National Smelters and Recyclers Association, Pakistan as well as The President of Gujranwala Chamber of Commerce and Industry.
